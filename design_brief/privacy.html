Unmanned Aircraft Systems: Privacy & Safety

Safety Concerns

UAS pose a concern when in open airspace, as they may not be capable of avoiding, or even recognizing, other aircrafts, buildings, people, or animals. Even if it can recognize and avoid them, a UAS can still crash into them due to malfunction of human error. Suppose a UAS avoids crashing into anything but ground, the debris can still hit people, animals, and property, causing serious damages or can be potentially fatal. UAS can be used to fly contraband across borders and into prisons. They may also be hacked and could potentially fall victim to cyberterrorism, and weapons can be added to them, further allowing them to be used for terrorist activities

Not only do the safety issues prove to be detrimental to future drone use, but so do the privacy concerns associated with their use.

Privacy Concerns

Due to the privacy concerns related to UAS usage and the fear that it impedes on the the fourth amendment, many states have put forth laws that regulate their use. Many of the laws prevent UAS from recording or photographing personal or private activity, from photographing private property, and from being used for surveillance. These laws eliminate their use by paparazzi and “peeping Toms.” Recently, the FAA (Federal Aviation Administration) has also enacted a law which requires that any owner of a UAS register their aircraft. Having an active list of registered UAS allows the FAA & the states to more effectively enforce the other laws regarding their use. You can register your UAS on the FAA's website.
