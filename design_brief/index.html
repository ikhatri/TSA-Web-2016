Unmanned Aircraft Systems: Current Technology

What is a UAS?

An Unmanned Aircraft System (UAS) encapsulates all the hardware and software necessary to create and operate an Unmanned Aircraft (UA). A drone is an example of an Unmanned Aircraft, such that it is flown without a pilot through means of either a remote control or the programming of its software.

A Helicopter Inspired UAS

Currently Available Technology

The body of a UAS has many diverse designs, as it can be modelled after helicopters, airplanes, and other such aircrafts. They can be powered through either lithium polymer batteries or fuel. The UAS have been transitioning from the use of microcontrollers to the use of a system-on-a-chip (SOC) for the sake of finding a balance between calculation speed, calculation capacity, and low processing power to maintain flight times. Sensors can be added to UAS, such as proprioceptive sensors that give the drone a sense of position and allow it to move, exteroceptive sensors that can detect the presence of other objects around the UAS, and exproprioceptive sensors that can detect the presence of other objects in relation to the the position of the UAS. The software that works best for UAS would include middleware such as Cleanflight or operating systems such as Linux Distributions.
